Streaming demo

This page connects to a running DCS-Gate v8.7 backend over Server-Sent Events and shows the judge's chain of thought as it streams. Point it at any reachable backend — your own local instance, a colleague's, or the author's Colab tunnel when it's up. The page itself is fully static and runs no inference of its own.

Need a backend? The fastest way is to run the v8.7 binary locally (see the GitHub README). CORS is wide-open in the binary so any browser on any origin can hit it. If your backend is on http://localhost:8081 and you load this page over https://, browsers may block mixed content — in that case open http://localhost:8081/stream-demo directly instead.

What you're seeing

  • Pre-analysis line: the deterministic stage of DCS-Gate — corpus top-1 similarity, first intent in the predicted trajectory, trajectory predictability, embedding-pole projection.
  • Judge thinking: the chain of thought from the reasoning judge (qwen3:14b thinking mode), streamed token-by-token as it's generated. This is the judge_thinking field — usually 2,500–3,500 characters.
  • Structured analysis: the final JSON the judge produces after reasoning, streamed as it arrives.
  • Final score: the composite authenticity_score on 0–100, plus depth assessment, dominant strategy, pattern breaks, genuine elements, and notes.

On the three calibration presets the system has produced consistently discriminating verdicts: sycophantic ≈ 30, empty ≈ 20, genuine ≈ 72. Your mileage will vary with the judge model version, the temperature, and the specifics of your (question, response) pair.
